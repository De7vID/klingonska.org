A Quick Reference to Adverbials in Klingon

The adverbial normally preceedes the object–verb–subject construction, but is sometimes itself preceeded by other elements (typically a time element such as at three o'clock or tomorrow). However it may also be placed between the object and the verb, if the object is topicalized with the -'e' suffix.

Adverbials may be used as exclamations functioning as more or less a sentence in their own right (for example: wej Don't do it yet!, nom Quickly – come on!, nIteb Do it alone!).

jay' intensely (invective) (adv) Comes at the end of a sentence. [TKDa p.177]
neH only, merely, just (adv) Follows the verb or noun it modifies. [TKD pp. 56–57, TKW]

The two above adverbials display a more irregular behaviour than the others. jay' is always placed last in the sentence an has the effect of intensifying it (for example: tIbach jay' Shoot him, goddamnit!). neH may be used after a verb to trivialize it's effect (for example: vIqIppu' neH I only hit him.) or after a noun to mean only (for example: jonta' neH just the engine).
